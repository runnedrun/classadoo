FAQ

How did Classadoo start?

The Classadoo co-founders are David Gaynor (CEO, co-founder and teacher) and Ari Chae (co-founder and teacher). David and Ari previously co-founded the nonprofit, School Shaped. Through listening to teachers, David and Ari discovered that coding lessons often lack context: coding should be a means to an end, not just an end. David and Ari decided to combine their tech and academic experience for Classadoo, a digital classroom that engages students with opportunities to create websites that are inspired by their passions. Classadoo teaches students to be creators, not just coders.

Who are the Classadoo teachers?

We are passionate educators from the biggest names in tech: Twitter, SpaceX, Uber, Google and more. Check out our teachers page for more info.

What is the Classadoo course structure?

A Classadoo teacher will lead a weekly group session remotely for one hour. They provide help between sessions if needed and are available for scheduled 1:1 chats, responding to email within 24 hours. Level 2 and Advanced students will create projects that require some work outside of class.

If you are interested in Classadoo but would like to customize the structure to suit your classroom's learning needs, just email learn@classadoo.com.

What does a Classadoo session look like?

We teach 2 types of classes: local and distributed sessions. Students are required to install the Classadoo chrome extension for both session types. This plugin allows our teachers to track students' progress. The Classadoo teachers and course content is the same for both session types.

A local session is similar to a traditional course at school - except the Classadoo teacher is onscreen instead of there in-person. All you need is 1.) a screen that connects to a computer and 2.) a computer with internet access for each student. Local sessions are possible when there are enough interested students in a geographic area. Schools and homeschool groups usually organize local sessions.

A distributed session, on the other hand, is a session that occurs without a physical classroom for students from different geographic areas. Students log into a secure live video stream from anywhere. They can ask teachers questions though audio, though their video is hidden from the Classadoo teacher and other participants.

Do students work together?

Yes. We think collaboration is an important skill, so we encourage students to solve problems in groups during our local sessions. These sessions include a group project that students work on over multiple sessions.

We are still working on ways to include group work in our distributed sessions.

Do all the students need to be there in person?

Only for local sessions. Students are remote for our distributed sessions.
